Particulate Concentration

Coarse Particulates (2.5 - 10μm)

Sources
  • Natural: pollen, dust, sea spray
  • Human: Construction & Agriculture byproducts
Health Impacts
  • Respiratory Problems, including worsening asthma
  • Cardiovascular Issues, including increased risk of heart attack

Safe Range: <50μg/m3 short term, <20μg/m3 long term

Fine Particulates (< 2.5μm)

Sources
  • Burning fossil fuels
  • Wood burning and cooking
  • Industrial & Agricultural byproducts
Health Impacts
  • Fine particles pose the gretest risk to health
  • Respiratory Problems
  • Inerased risk of heart attack
Environmental Impacts
  • Contribute to acid rain & the acidification of waterways
  • Damage ecosystems & ecological diversity

Safe Range: Should not exceed 5μg/m3

Ultra-Fine Particulates (< 100nm)

Sources
  • Organic Compounds, nitrates, sulfates, and trace metals
  • Combustion: Diesel engines, cooking, wood burning
Health Impacts
  • UFPs can easily enter the respiratory system and even penetrate cells
  • Can cause systemic inflammation
  • Cardiovasular disease, hypertension, and even neurological issues

Safe Range: Should not exceed 5μg/m3
